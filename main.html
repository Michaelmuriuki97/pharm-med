Smart Clinical Support System

Preventing Adverse Drug Events Among Elderly Patients on Polypharmacy in Nairobi County

About the Study

The purpose of this study is to design and evaluate a smart clinical support system that detects and flags potentially harmful drug combinations in real time, thereby preventing adverse drug events (ADEs) among elderly patients (aged 60+) on polypharmacy within Nairobi County hospitals.

Gachoki Michael Muriuki

Objectives

  1. Assess the current prevalence of polypharmacy and ADEs among elderly patients in participating hospitals.
  2. Identify the most common drug–drug interactions and inappropriate medications using Beers Criteria, STOPP/START guidelines, and pharmacy records.
  3. Design a smart support system (AI or rule-based) that flags harmful drug combinations in real time.
  4. Pilot the system with real patient prescription data to evaluate accuracy in predicting high-risk medication profiles.
  5. Compare system performance with manual pharmacist assessments to determine sensitivity, specificity, and error reduction.
  6. Evaluate healthcare professionals' attitudes, perceptions, and ease of use through structured interviews and questionnaires.
  7. Develop practical recommendations to implement and scale the system across Nairobi County health facilities.

Methodology Overview

This study will employ a mixed-methods approach combining retrospective prescription data analysis, AI model development, pilot testing, and qualitative user feedback to refine the support system.
